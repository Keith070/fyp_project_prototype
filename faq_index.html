Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about blockchain voting

Blockchain-based voting is a digital voting system that uses blockchain technology to ensure transparency, security, and immutability of votes. Each vote is recorded as a transaction on a decentralized ledger, preventing tampering and providing a verifiable audit trail while maintaining voter privacy.

Blockchain secures votes through several mechanisms:

  • Encryption: Votes are encrypted end-to-end
  • Decentralization: No single point of failure
  • Consensus mechanisms: Multiple nodes must verify transactions
  • Immutability: Once recorded, votes cannot be altered
  • Transparency: All transactions are publicly verifiable

  2. Confirm it hasn't been altered
  3. Check that it was included in the final tally
